## 🌎 About FLISoL
The **Latin American Free Software Installation Festival (FLISoL)** is the largest Free Software dissemination event in Latin America. Held annually since 2005, it takes place simultaneously in hundreds of cities across the region. 

Its primary mission is to promote the use of **Free Software**, bringing its philosophy, technical advancements, and social impact to the general public. During the festival, volunteers install open-source operating systems and applications for attendees, alongside workshops, talks, and exhibitions.

## 🎯 Purpose of this Repository
This repository contains the official **Visual Identity Manual** and design assets for FLISoL. It was created to provide local organizers, designers, and community members with high-quality, consistent materials for digital and print media.

The goal is to maintain a professional and unified aesthetic that reflects the innovative and collaborative spirit of the Free Software movement.

## 🎨 Visual Guidelines

### 1. The Logo
The FLISoL logo represents freedom and community. 
- **Safe Zone:** Always maintain a clear margin around the logo equivalent to 50% of the symbol's width.
- **Minimum Size:** For digital use, the logo should not be smaller than `80px` in width.

### 2. Color Palette
Our identity uses a vibrant and modern palette to stand out:
- **Primary Green:** `#78B13F` (Symbolizing growth and freedom)
- **Deep Slate:** `#2D2D2D` (For readability and structure)
- **Accent Blue:** `#3498DB` (Representing technology and community)

### 3. Typography
We prioritize open-source fonts to stay true to our philosophy:
- **Primary:** [Montserrat](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Montserrat) (Bold/Semi-Bold for headings)
- **Secondary:** [Open Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Open+Sans) (Regular for body text)

## 🚀 How to Use
To use these assets in your local FLISoL event:
1. **Clone the repo:** `git clone https://github.com/foxbit/Manual-de-identidade-Flisol.git`
2. Browse the `/Logos` folder for vector files.
3. Follow the color specifications in the `/Colors` directory to ensure brand consistency across your printed materials.
